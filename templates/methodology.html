Methodology

Corpus Selection

The analysis is based on literary texts from three major languages:

  • English: Literature from British, American, and other English-speaking regions (1810-1960)
  • Hindi: Literature from the Hindi heartland of India (1810-1960)
  • Tamil: Literature from Tamil Nadu, India, and Sri Lanka (1810-1960)

Each corpus was selected to represent the major literary movements and cultural contexts of each language tradition.

Emotion Classification Model

We employ a multi-class emotion detection model based on natural language processing techniques:

  • Emotion Categories: 11 emotions - Anger, Contempt, Disgust, Fear, Frustration, Gratitude, Joy, Love, Neutral, Sadness, Surprise
  • Method: Machine learning classifier trained on annotated literary text segments
  • Validation: Cross-validated against human expert annotations
Intensity Measurement

Each text segment receives a probability score (0-1) for each emotion category. The intensity is calculated as the weighted sum of these probabilities across all segments in a given decade.

Temporal Aggregation

Texts are grouped by decade (1810s, 1820s, ..., 1960s). For each decade, we calculate:

  • Mean Intensity: Average emotion score across all texts in that decade
  • Rolling Average: 3-decade centered moving average for trend smoothing
Cross-Cultural Normalization

To enable meaningful comparison across languages, emotion scores are normalized proportionally within each language dataset, allowing us to compare relative emotional emphasis rather than absolute values.

Correlation Analysis

Pearson correlation coefficients are calculated between all emotion pairs to identify emotional co-occurrence patterns.

Significance Testing

Independent samples t-tests are used to determine statistically significant differences in emotion intensities between languages.

  • p < 0.05: * (significant)
  • p < 0.01: ** (highly significant)
  • p < 0.001: *** (very highly significant)
Trend Analysis

Linear regression is applied to identify overall trends in emotional trajectories over time. The R² value indicates the strength of the trend.

  • Corpus Size: The size and representativeness of each language corpus may vary
  • Translation Effects: Emotion detection may be affected by translation quality
  • Historical Bias: Literary canon selection may reflect historical biases
  • Temporal Resolution: Decade-level aggregation may miss shorter-term variations
  • Cultural Specificity: Some emotions may be more culturally embedded than others
