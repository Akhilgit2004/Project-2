Glossary

Correlation

A statistical measure that describes the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables. Values range from -1 (perfect negative) to +1 (perfect positive).

Cross-Correlation

Measures the similarity between two time series as a function of the displacement (lag) of one relative to the other. Used to identify delayed relationships between emotions.

Decade

A 10-year period used as the basic unit of temporal analysis. Our data spans from the 1810s to the 1960s.

Emotion Intensity

A numerical value (0-1) representing the strength of a particular emotion detected in a text, based on the frequency and context of emotional language.

Heatmap

A data visualization technique that uses color coding to represent different values. In our dashboard, it shows emotion intensities across decades, with warmer colors indicating higher intensity.

Lag

In time series analysis, the number of time periods by which one variable is shifted relative to another to measure correlation.

Literary Period

A defined era in literary history characterized by distinctive themes, styles, and conventions. Examples include Romantic (1810-1840), Victorian (1840-1900), and Modern (1910-1960).

Mirror Hypothesis

The theory that emotional content in literature reflects the emotional climate of the society and historical events of the time period in which it was produced.

Pearson Correlation

The most common measure of linear correlation between two variables, calculated as the covariance divided by the product of standard deviations.

Radar Chart

Also called a spider chart or polar chart. A graphical method of displaying multivariate data in the form of a two-dimensional chart of quantitative variables.

Rolling Average

A smoothing technique that calculates the average of a subset of neighboring data points. We use a 3-decade centered window to smooth temporal variations.

R² (R-squared)

A statistical measure that represents the proportion of the variance for a dependent variable that's explained by an independent variable in a regression model.

Statistical Significance

A result that is unlikely to have occurred by chance alone. We use p-values to determine significance, with p < 0.05 typically considered significant.

Time Series

A sequence of data points indexed in time order, typically measured at successive points (in our case, decades).

Trendline

A line that best represents the overall direction of data points in a scatter plot, typically calculated using linear regression.

Violin Plot

A combination of a box plot and a kernel density plot. It shows the distribution of data across different categories, with wider sections indicating higher probability.
