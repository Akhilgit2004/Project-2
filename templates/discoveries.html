Interesting Discoveries

Explore fascinating patterns and insights from our cross-cultural emotion analysis.

Joy Peaks in the 1920s

Across all three languages, Joy shows a significant peak during the 1920s - the "Roaring Twenties" era. This suggests a global mood of optimism reflected in literature.

Fear Spikes During Wars

Fear intensity increases significantly during World War I (1910s) and World War II (1940s) periods across all cultures, validating the Mirror Hypothesis.

Love: Tamil Literature Leads

Tamil literature consistently shows higher Love intensities compared to English and Hindi across all time periods, suggesting a stronger romantic tradition in Tamil literary expression.

Emotion Cycles

Emotional patterns appear to cycle approximately every 30-40 years across all cultures, suggesting generational shifts in literary emotional content.

Joy-Sadness Inverse Correlation

Joy and Sadness show strong negative correlation across all languages - when joy increases, sadness decreases, and vice versa.

Victorian Era: Most Similar

The Victorian period (1840-1900) shows the highest cross-cultural similarity in emotional profiles, suggesting shared global influences during industrialization.
